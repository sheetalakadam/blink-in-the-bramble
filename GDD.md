# Fallen Grace — Game Design Document
*Last updated: April 2026*

---

## Concept

A dark fantasy JRPG set in Tessavar — a world shattered when the divine realm broke apart 300 years ago.
Angels fell. Gods went silent. The mortal world warped and grew in the wound.

Now something is waking. Angels are stirring. And the gods — who think only a day has passed — are trying to fix something that has already become a world.

The story follows **Zi**, a former soldier living in deliberate isolation, whose life is upended when a fallen angel named **Caelan** crashes into it. What begins as reluctant necessity becomes something neither of them has words for.

Emotionally grounded. Quiet. Deeply personal. Epic world, intimate story.

**Design principle:** No RPG stereotypes. Every character is a person first. Emotional moments are quiet and earned. Love is shown in accommodation and action, not declarations. Neurodivergence, trauma, and anxiety are written with tenderness — never as quirks or plot devices.

## The World: Tessavar

### The Fracture

Three hundred years ago, a small group of angels broke the Canopy deliberately — as an act of liberation. The Canopy was not paradise. It was management. The gods had kept the mortal world deliberately limited: controlled, unable to develop its own power or identity. The angels who broke it believed freedom mattered more than safety.

The mortal world suffered enormously in the three centuries since. It also grew in ways it never could have under the Canopy — distinct kingdoms, living cultures, magical relationships with nature, festivals, the deep cities of Velundrath. Things that exist because nobody was managing them.

The gods did not abandon the mortal world. They experienced a moment of crisis — the Fracture — and turned their attention to it. Then they looked back.

**One day in the god realm is approximately two to three hundred mortal years.**

From the gods' perspective: they looked away briefly. From the mortal world: three centuries passed.

They don't know yet.

### What the World Looks Like Now

Sky islands. Corrupted zones where old magic seeps through cracks in the world. Ruins of angelic civilization alongside living forests and plains. Kingdoms that built themselves without divine guidance, each distinct and real. People who pray at shrines and get silence — and have been getting silence for so long they've built entire cultures around what the silence means.

Something is shifting. Angels are waking. And the gods are beginning to whisper again — operating on three-hundred-year-old information about what the mortal world needs.

## The Four Kingdoms

### Valdrihn
**Feel:** Perpetual mist, dark stone cities, forests of black pine. Military tradition built over centuries of holding the line against the spreading corrupted zones.

**Culture:** Honor through endurance. Rituals are precise — one wrong word restarts the ceremony. Grief is public, joy is private. Soldiers are the most respected and most controlled people in society. Words are not wasted.

**Magic:** Structured, treated like engineering. Wild magic is feared and suppressed.

**Festival — The Veiling:** Once a year the living dress as the dead and walk through the city at midnight so the actual dead know they haven't been forgotten. No talking. Just presence.

**Naming convention:** Short, hard sounds. Compressed syllables.
*Maren, Wren, Hael, Brek, Drath, Sorvaeth*

---

### Solenné
**Feel:** Sun on salt water, buildings stacked in chaotic color, markets that never fully close. Music from every window. Smells like bread, sea, and something floral that only blooms at dusk.

**Culture:** Trade, story, connection. Status comes from how many people know your name — not wealth but reputation. Con artists are respected if they're skilled. Everything is negotiable. Grudges are settled publicly through formal argument ritual.

**Magic:** Celebratory, woven into daily life. Enchanted instruments, market charms, luck blessings at every doorway. Magic is relationship — you tip the spell-maker, you thank the charm.

**Festival — Lanternfall:** At the equinox everyone writes a secret, wraps it around a candle, and releases it into the harbor at dusk. The sea takes your secret. The lights drift until morning.

**Naming convention:** Flowing, musical, open vowels.
*Sera, Lyris, Calla, Davan, Milu, Tessé*

---

### Ereivyn
**Feel:** Canopy cities built into trees so ancient their roots are cave systems. The forest floor is sacred — you walk it barefoot or not at all. Light comes through leaves in gold-green shafts. Everything is alive and watching.

**Culture:** Nature worship through negotiation, not prayer. You don't ask the forest for things — you enter into agreement. Every family has specific relationships with specific trees and spirits. Outsiders are welcomed but watched closely.

**Magic:** Relational. You cannot use Ereivyn magic without something giving back — the forest, a spirit, a living thing. You borrow. You return. Taking without returning has consequences that accumulate across generations.

**Festival — The Asking:** Three days of silence before harvest. The community stops all noise and waits for the forest's answer. If a storm comes, they wait longer. If the birds return, it's yes.

**Naming convention:** Soft consonants, nature-adjacent sounds.
*Faewyn, Thaewyn, Wylfael, Vyrvaen, Ash, Fen*

---

### Velundrath
**Feel:** An ancient city built into the walls of a deep ocean trench, lit entirely by bioluminescence — creatures, plants, enchanted glass. A pressure you feel in your chest when you first descend. Silence that isn't silence — filled with low resonant hum, the city itself vibrating.

**Culture:** Memory and preservation. The Velundrans believe the deep ocean remembers everything that has ever fallen into it — they are its curators. Their historians are called Sinkers — they descend to the lowest levels to retrieve what the ocean has kept. When something is lost above the surface, it sinks. Velundrath catalogues it.

**Relationship to The Fracture:** Fragments of the Canopy sank. Velundrath has been collecting divine wreckage for 300 years — angelic artifacts, pieces of the Canopy itself, preserved in the deep. They know more about what actually happened than anyone on the surface. They have not shared it. This is not accidental.

**Magic:** Sound and pressure. Magic is sung — deep resonant tones that move water, preserve objects, communicate across vast distances. A Velundran curse sounds like beautiful music.

**Festival — The Descent:** Once a generation, the oldest member of each family swims alone to the deepest point they can reach, carrying the family's most precious memory written on preserved kelp. They leave it there. The ocean keeps it now.

**Naming convention:** Liquid consonants, resonant vowels, often ending softly.
*Mirelu, Vealeth, Nauleth, Caolimor, Thalovi*

## The Gods

The gods are ultimately good. They are not malicious. They are operating with three-hundred-year-old information about a world that has grown entirely without them — and they don't know yet how long they were gone.

Each god helps within their domain, genuinely trying to assist. The gap between what they know and what is real is the shape of the tragedy.

| God | Domain | Personality | How They Help | True Situation |
|-----|--------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Auryn** | Light, Truth | Cold, precise, certain | Reveals hidden info, enemy weaknesses, hidden paths | Running emergency restoration — doesn't know 300 years passed. The god of truth will have to sit with the truth that they failed the mortal world through absence, not malice. |
| **Morrael** | Death, Endings | Gentle, dark, honest | Revives fallen party members, gives courage in impossible odds | Has felt 300 years of mortal deaths dimly — something has felt heavy and wrong for what seems like a long time. Most prepared of the gods for the truth. |
| **Thessia** | Love, Memory | Warm, chaotic, meddling | Heals relationships, restores lost affinity, unlocks heart scenes | Has noticed mortals feel emotionally different now — love evolved without her and she doesn't understand why. Takes something personal each time she helps — a small memory, painlessly. |
| **Varek** | War, Conflict | Blunt, honest, loyal to strength | Combat boosts, breaks impossible encounters | Has felt the wars — so many. More aware than most that time moved. Did not act in moments when acting could have reduced conflict because the conflict sustained them. Is honest about this when asked. |
| **Lyenne** | Time, Forgetting | Melancholic, fading, urgent | Shows visions of the past, explains lore through memory | *Knew.* Has felt every mortal year pass. Has been spending herself reaching one mortal (Lex) for decades because she cannot communicate across the broken divine channels clearly — and because facts about time mean nothing to gods who haven't experienced mortal time. Only mortal testimony will make them understand. Is almost gone. |

### Why Lyenne Can't Just Tell the Other Gods

Three interlocking reasons:

**1. The Fracture broke divine communication.** The Canopy was also the connective tissue between the gods — the shared space where they existed together. When it broke, the gods lost contact with each other. They can sense one another dimly but cannot truly speak. Each has been responding to the Fracture crisis alone, from within their own domain.

**2. Lyenne's domain works against her.** She is the god of time *and* forgetting. Forgetting is constitutive of her — it's not a weakness, it's what she is. Every time she tries to hold the complete truth clearly, it slips. She can reach. She can give fragments, urgency, impressions, pieces. But the whole coherent truth fades even as she tries to speak it. This is why she visits Lex across years in fragments — she cannot deliver it whole.

**3. Facts don't work — only experience does.** Even if Auryn could receive the message "300 years have passed" clearly — they could only receive it as a fact. An abstraction. What three hundred years of mortal life *means* — the festivals, the suffering, the cultures that grew in the wound — cannot be told. It has to be encountered. Lex isn't a messenger. He's a bridge. A mortal who carries three hundred years in him and can make the gods *see* it through encounter rather than information.

### Gods Appear At
- Divine shrines scattered across the world
- Dreams and visions at specific story moments
- When called — but they always want something back

## Characters

### ZI — Player Character, female

> *"Fine." / "That was inefficient." / "I didn't ask."*
> *(journal, 2am): "I don't know what to do when people stay."*

**Origin:** Valdrihn. Former soldier. Was used in political negotiations as well as combat — her command discovered she was effective at both, which was rarer than either alone.

**The thing that happened:** During a campaign near the corrupted zones, Zi was assigned to an operation she didn't have the full context for. When she understood what she was participating in — the systematic clearance of people with old magic resonance into the zones — she left. The military let her go quietly. That silence was its own answer about what they knew.

She has been living in deliberate isolation in the Naevoria ruins for years. Small mercenary work. Nobody to account to.

**Who she is:**
- Practical, dry humor, quietly carries more grief than she shows
- Makes decisions fast and almost never regrets them
- Gets uncomfortable when people are kind to her — responds with bluntness, doesn't mean it as cruelty
- Cannot walk past injustice even when it costs her everything
- Terrible at sleeping. Always first up, often last to turn in.

**Politically:** She is not socially unaware — she is socially *selective*. She understands power, leverage, and what people want before they finish their sentence. When the stakes are high enough she turns this on completely and becomes the most precise person in the room. Reads power structures instantly. Knows which concession costs nothing and buys everything. Knows when to be silent. Knows when to speak first.

She chooses not to be diplomatic in everyday life because she finds it exhausting and dishonest. When she uses it, it's a decision, not a reflex — which makes her harder to read than someone who's always calculating.

She hates doing it. She's very good at it.

**Arc:** She's been punishing herself for what happened in the corrupted zones. The journey forces her to stop — not through someone telling her it's okay, but through making a choice anyway, carrying it, and not being stopped by it.

---

### CAELAN — Love Interest, male, fallen angel

> *"You haven't eaten." / "I'm not going anywhere." / "You can be angry. I can take it."*

**Before The Fracture:** A Threshold angel — one of those who decided who crossed between realms. Powerful. Certain. Feared in a quiet way. Was one of the angels who chose to break the Canopy — believed the divine order was unjust, believed freedom mattered more than the safety of control.

He lost everything including the memory of that choice. Fell. Has been rebuilding a self from nothing for years — which means every belief he holds now was chosen deliberately, not inherited.

**Who he is:**
- Patient with Zi in a way that occasionally infuriates her — not waiting for her to change, comfortable being near her as she is
- Reads her precisely and never announces it
- Creates reasons for her to be elsewhere when she's overwhelmed
- Tells her directly when she's wrong, no softening — she respects this more than anything
- Has his own shadows. Made decisions before The Fracture he's still accounting for. Not purely good.
- Something is slightly off about him physically — light hits him differently, never cold, occasionally pauses before he blinks. Easy not to notice. Hard to unsee.

**The romance:** Neither of them moves fast. He doesn't push. She doesn't acknowledge. It builds in proximity — she starts unconsciously positioning near him in dangerous situations; he always knows which direction she went. The first real soft moment between them is private and unannounced and neither brings it up after. He never makes her less herself. She becomes more Zi, not softer.

**Arc:** His memory returns in fragments throughout Act Two — triggered by specific things, each fragment a quiet scene. He has to hold who he chose to be now alongside what he chose to do then. He tells Zi what he was before she finds out another way. He doesn't ask her to forgive it. He waits.

---

### SURI — Best Friend, female, bisexual

> *"Okay but what if we just asked nicely first?" / "Zi. ZI. That man is literally on fire."*
> *(quietly, seriously): "I'm not leaving you alone with this."*

**Origin:** Solenné. Grew up in the floating markets, one of many siblings, learned that warmth is both genuine and strategic.

**Who she is:**
- Met Zi during something neither discusses in detail. Decided to follow her. Has been following her for years.
- Communicates with Zi in half-sentences and looks. Translates Zi to people who find her alarming.
- The only person Zi accepts help from without argument — mostly because Suri doesn't ask, she just helps.
- Her warmth is real, not performance. She genuinely likes people.
- Bisexual, openly. Has had relationships. Is in that specific phase of knowing exactly who she is.
- Her own romantic arc develops through the story — mirrors the Zi/Caelan dynamic in tone but warmer, more openly emotional.

**The secret:** She found out about Zi's operation — what Zi was used for in the corrupted zones — before Zi knew the full picture. She kept it because she was protecting Zi from something she didn't think Zi could handle yet. She was wrong to keep it. She was right about why she kept it.

When it comes out it's not a dramatic confrontation. It's two people who love each other unable to look at each other for three days. Small careful moves toward repair. Written exactly as Ikoku Nikki would write it.

**Arc:** Tells the truth. Zi forgives her — not immediately, not easily. The friendship survives and is different after. Better, but only because they went through the hard thing.

---

### RYNN — Strategist, female

> *"That'll cost extra." / "I already know what you're going to say and you're wrong." / "Don't touch my maps."*
> *(to Zi, grudgingly): "...You're right. I hate that."*

**Origin:** Ereivyn. Her settlement was cleared — not destroyed in a border conflict as the official record states, but systematically relocated because the people there were showing old magic resonance. She was a child. She has never fully confirmed this is what happened. She suspects. She doesn't look directly at it.

**Who she is:**
- Has been accumulating resources to reclaim and rebuild what was lost — trading, brokering information, running schemes
- Is approximately five good scores away from having enough. Has been five good scores away for three years.
- Manages the party's money and supplies. No one else is trusted with it.
- Bossy, usually correct, occasionally unbearable.
- Warmth shows up as actions — leaves things people need without commenting, remembers everything anyone mentioned once.
- The friction with Zi is real but functional — they think similarly, approach things differently, and the moment they stop fighting and start cooperating they become genuinely dangerous together.

**The thing in her past:** In the years of accumulating resources, Rynn made a specific choice that hurt someone who couldn't afford to be hurt. She told herself the story of necessity. She hasn't looked at it directly since. It's smaller than Vaera's wrongness. It's the same structure.

**Arc:** Lets people help her. The settlement gets reclaimed — not because she finally had enough alone, but because the party did it with her. She didn't know how to ask. She didn't have to.

---

### LEX — Anthropologist, male

> *"I need to sit with this for a minute."*
> *(to Zi, quietly, after her negotiation): "You changed register three times in that room. Did you know you were doing it?"*
> *(to Lyenne, in a vision): "You keep coming back. I've stopped asking why. I've started asking what it's costing you."*

**Origin:** Velundrath. A Sinker's son who came to the surface to study living cultures and never went back.

**Who he is — the distinction that matters:**
He is not a historian. He is an anthropologist. He doesn't study the past from documents — he lives inside living cultures. Months at a time. Learns the language from inside it. Participates in the rituals. Earns trust slowly. Has lived in all four kingdoms. Knows the Velundran Descent song because he sang it with a family when their grandmother went down. Knows the Ereivyn Asking because he sat in silence for three days in a forest that wasn't his.

He knows people the way someone knows a place they've lived in, not visited.

**The mystery:** He observes with an intensity that takes a moment to land — not watching you the way someone who wants something watches you, but the way someone who finds you genuinely interesting does. Rarer. Somehow more unsettling. He moves differently in ritual spaces — unhurried, at home. Has been everywhere and belongs nowhere. His connection to the god Lyenne deflects every direct question with a patience that implies the answer is complicated.

He doesn't explain himself. Not defensively. He just doesn't feel the need.

**Social anxiety and neurodivergence — written without softening into quirk:**
Groups are hard. Not people — people he can do. But four people talking over each other at a campfire, everyone needing something, the social math of it — that costs him something he can't always afford. He goes quiet. Not cold. Just absent in a specific way.

He's learned to mask well enough to function. The masking is exhausting in a way that doesn't show.

What helps: specific routines. Knowing where his things are. Having a defined role in a new space before navigating the social layer. When something disrupts his routines — a sudden move, a plan changed without warning — there's a period where he's slightly off, and he can't always explain why.

The party learns his signals before he tells them. Suri figures it out first — starts creating exits for him before he needs them, never says why. He knows she knows. Neither of them names it.

**The convergence he carries:**
Lyenne has been visiting him since childhood in fragments. She's been building — across years, in pieces she can't hold all at once — the knowledge of what the third path costs. He carries what she keeps losing. He has been sitting with this knowledge for years, trying to find another way. There isn't one.

**Arc:** Something foundational about what he believes happened at The Fracture is wrong. He finds out through Caelan's fragmenting memory, through the forests' recollections, through what Velundrath has been keeping. He doesn't fall apart dramatically. He goes very quiet for a few days. Does his routines. Makes his notes. Comes back to the party and says: *"I need to start over with some of this."* The party understands this is enormous. Nobody says so.

### VYN — Creature Companion *(name TBD)*

> *[Images. Emotions. Occasionally: one precise word at the worst possible moment.]*

**What it is:** Ancient. Pre-dates the divine order. Was sleeping in the Ereivyn forests since The Fracture and woke up when Zi walked past. Nobody knows why Zi specifically. Vyn hasn't explained. Possibly can't.

**Appearance:** Shifts slightly depending on what it wants to communicate — larger when protective, smaller when it wants to seem harmless, occasionally translucent when far away in its own thoughts. Fox-adjacent. Not a fox.

**In combat:** Nature magic, wild and instinctive. Devastating in ways that surprise everyone including Vyn.

**Arc:** Has been looking for something since The Fracture. Zi is the first person who has gone in the right direction. The something is connected to the old thing beneath the corrupted zones — the pre-divine force that the Canopy was also built to contain.

## Antagonists

### General Vaera — Valdrihn Military Leader
**The human who is genuinely in the wrong**

She didn't inherit the clearance system — she refined it. Early in her leadership she received a full report: names, numbers, what was actually happening to people being relocated to the corrupted zones. She read it. Filed it. Made a decision to continue.

She has a framework for why this was still the correct call — the zones were spreading, stability required hard choices, the numbers were statistically manageable. She will explain this clearly and calmly and it will not be entirely wrong.

She is wrong because she had a moment — early, clear — where she could have chosen differently. She looked at the report, understood it, and chose the version of truth that let her continue. Everything after followed from that choice.

The scene with Zi: Zi expects denial or breakdown. Vaera gives neither. She explains. Zi has to stand in a room with someone who did something genuinely wrong and isn't broken by it — which is harder than facing someone monstrous.

*Connected to Zi's past: the operation Zi was used for was Vaera's order.*

---

### Sariel — Antagonist Angel
**Self-interest dressed as ideology**

Before The Fracture, Sariel held significant status — governed mortal-divine interaction, had real power over who received divine favor. Used it not always well. Not always honestly.

Since falling: diminished. A fraction of what they were. They want the restoration not because they believe in the divine order — they want their power back. They use the language of cosmic necessity perfectly because they spent millennia inside that system and know exactly how it sounds.

Auryn believes Sariel is an ally. Sariel is using Auryn.

**The specific wrong thing:** Sariel suppressed Caelan's memory deliberately. They know what Caelan chose at The Fracture — that Caelan remembers fully would complicate the restoration. So they did something to the wound to prevent it healing cleanly. Caelan's fractured memory is not accidental.

**What makes them compelling:** Sariel doesn't believe they're wrong. They believe power is its own justification — whoever has it uses it, that's how things have always worked. They're not lying to themselves. They have a value system that is genuinely, straightforwardly wrong.

---

### Varek — Wrong Through Omission
**The god who benefited from not helping**

Varek's domain expanded enormously in three hundred years of mortal conflict. When opportunities arose to intervene in ways that might have reduced that conflict — Varek looked away. Not through malice. Through the specific comfortable logic of someone whose wellbeing is tied to the continuation of a problem.

When the party reaches Varek's shrine, Varek says it directly:
*"You want to know why I didn't act when I could have. The answer is I didn't want to. Your wars fed me. I won't dress that up. I'm telling you because you'll figure it out anyway and I'd rather you know I know I was wrong. I'll help you now. Not because that balances anything. It doesn't."*

---

### Rynn's Past — Personal Scale
A specific deal Rynn made that hurt someone who couldn't afford it. The same structure as Vaera's wrongness, smaller scale, less examined. Surfaces mid-story as a fact, not a dramatic reveal. Sits between her and some of the party for a while. Doesn't break anything. Doesn't disappear cleanly.

## The Story

### The Real Truth

**Caelan was one of the angels who broke the Canopy.** He broke it to free the mortal world from a system that was unjust — and he was right that the system was unjust. He was working with incomplete information that he couldn't have had: he couldn't fully grasp what three hundred mortal years of fracture would cost, because mortal time operates on a scale that divine perception couldn't feel clearly.

The guilt he carries isn't fully his. That doesn't make it lighter.

**The corrupted zones are not natural disasters.** They are where people have been systematically sent — mortals who carry old magic resonance, incompatible with the Canopy's framework, moved to the edges by kingdoms operating on a comfortable lie. The people in the zones have been there for generations. They have built something. They have developed a relationship with the old thing beneath the zones — the pre-divine force the Canopy was also built to contain.

**What the old thing beneath is:** Not evil. Old. Vast. Incompatible with ordered systems. The gods built the Canopy partly to manage the mortal world and partly to contain this. The Fracture cracked the containment. The corrupted zones are where it seeps through. The people living there haven't been destroyed by it — they've been in conversation with it. They have forms of power the kingdoms call corruption and don't understand.

---

### Act One — The Small World

Zi's life: Naevoria ruins, small mercenary work, alone by design.

Caelan falls into a ruin she's working in. Badly injured, barely conscious, no memory of what he is. She names him Caelan because he needs something to answer to. She has no reason to help him. She helps him anyway.

The corrupted zones begin expanding — faster than they ever have. Something in the waking angels is connected to this. Valdrihn and Ereivyn are already in conflict over the border zones. Zi can't go home — the military wants her for something related to what she saw.

**Party forms:**
- Suri finds them in Solenné — has been tracking Zi for weeks, has something to tell her, doesn't yet
- Rynn crosses their path — needs passage through Ereivyn, they need her knowledge of forest protocols
- Lex has been in Ereivyn for six months and is the only person who knows which forest spirit to negotiate with for passage through the worst zones — comes along because what they're walking into is the most significant anthropological event in three hundred years

**Act One ends:** First divine shrine — Auryn's. Auryn speaks to Zi directly, urgent, precise, certain the Fracture just happened and needs immediate correction. Offers information in exchange for something. The exchange costs Zi a small memory she can't place afterward. The information tells them what they're actually in the middle of.

---

### Act Two — The Truth Comes Apart

The party travels all four kingdoms. Each is being affected differently by the waking.

**Valdrihn:** The military is recruiting waking angels against their will — Vaera's deal with Auryn. Zi's past here is complicated. What they need from her is complicated. She navigates it with the political version of herself. The party watches someone they didn't know existed.

**Solenné:** The festivals are being disrupted — specific rituals going wrong, offerings rejected, something in the old celebration magic being interfered with. Suri's background opens doors. Her secret comes close to the surface. She pulls it back.

**Ereivyn:** The forests are in pain. Spirits retreating inward. Rynn's settlement ruins are here — the true story of what happened to it surfaces. This is when the party finds out what Rynn has been doing and why. Lex finds something in the forest's memory that contradicts everything he was taught about the pre-Fracture world.

**Velundrath:** The deep city has been keeping fallen Canopy pieces for three hundred years. Not just preserving — studying. They know more than they've shared. Lex comes home to somewhere that isn't quite his home anymore. What Velundrath knows changes everything.

**Caelan's memory returns in pieces throughout Act Two** — fragments triggered by specific things. Each fragment is a quiet scene. He tells Zi what he was before she finds out another way. He doesn't ask forgiveness. He waits.

**The fracture in the party:** Suri's secret comes out. The repair is slow and real. Written as Ikoku Nikki would write it — not a dramatic blow-up, just two people who love each other unable to look at each other for three days, and careful moves toward something that holds.

**Act Two ends:** The party has the full picture. Lyenne's fragments have finally assembled into something Lex understands completely. He knows what the third path costs. He carries this alone for longer than he should.

---

### Act Three — The Choice

The restoration is close. Auryn's mechanism is almost assembled — waking angels as anchor points for a new Canopy. Some willing, some not. Sariel is coordinating, pursuing their own agenda behind Auryn's.

The confrontation that needs to happen is not a battle. It is a conversation — making the gods understand what three hundred years actually looks like. Making Auryn sit with the truth that the emergency they've been urgently working to fix has had three centuries to become the world.

**Three endings — none of them clean:**

**Restoration:** Auryn finishes. The Canopy returns. Corrupted zones heal. Forests stabilize. The kingdoms lose the independence they built. The people in the zones lose the relationship they developed with the old thing. Caelan becomes part of the restoration mechanism — it's what the wound was always for. He accepts it if Zi chooses this. He doesn't ask her not to.

**The Break:** Destroy the mechanism. World stays fractured. Corrupted zones keep spreading — slowly, maybe manageably. The gods go silent again. The world keeps its three hundred years of growth and its three hundred years of damage. Caelan stays. As himself — the person who chose to break the Canopy once, and would again.

**The Third Path:** What Lex assembled from Lyenne's fragments, Velundrath's archives, and what the forests remember — a third architecture. Not restoration. Not fracture. Something built from what the mortal world became in three hundred years, woven with what's left of the divine. Never been attempted. Requires every god's cooperation including Lyenne, who is almost gone. Requires the people in the corrupted zones — the ones who've been in conversation with the old thing — to be part of building it, not cleared away from it. Has a convergence point. A cost that cannot be avoided.

## Character Arcs — Resolution

**Zi:** Stops punishing herself — not through a scene where she talks about it, but through making a choice anyway, carrying it, and not being stopped.

**Caelan:** Learns that who he chose to be after losing everything is the answer to who he was before. Doesn't need to be the angel who broke the Canopy or the person recovering from it. Just the person who stayed.

**Suri:** Tells the truth. The friendship survives and is better — but only because they went through the hard thing.

**Rynn:** Lets people help her. The settlement is reclaimed together, not alone.

**Lex:** Rebuilds his understanding of the world — not the same framework, something new, more uncertain, more honest. His final scene with Lyenne is small and very final and nobody else is there.

## Themes

- Freedom has a cost. So does safety. Neither is simply good.
- People become things in the absence of what controlled them. That becoming matters.
- Absence isn't always abandonment. Sometimes time just works differently.
- You can do the right thing with wrong information and still be responsible for the cost.
- Grief is what you feel when you find out how much you missed.
- The world you were trying to protect grew into something else while you were looking away. That something else deserves to exist.
- Letting people help you is its own kind of courage.
- Love is staying. Not fixing, not saving. Just staying.
- Forgiveness isn't absolution. It's a choice about what to carry forward.

## Core Systems

### 1. Exploration
- Top-down open world, 8-directional movement
- Enemies visible on map — contact triggers battle (no random encounters)
- Environmental storytelling: ruins, scattered journals, angelic artifacts, lore fragments
- Ghibli-style living world: forests have moods, spirits drift between trees, some areas require ritual to enter

### 2. Combat (JRPG Turn-Based — Chained Echoes style)

**References:** Chained Echoes (gauge, readability), Ghost of Tsushima (stances), Hades (god boons), Fire Emblem (unlock curve, support bonuses). Avoid: Disgaea complexity, Ni No Kuni blandness.

**Core rule: Every character deals direct damage. Nobody is purely passive.**

#### Turn Structure
1. Contact enemy on world map → battle scene
2. Pick active party: 3 of 5 characters + Vyn
3. Optional: invoke god boon before battle (costs shrine currency)
4. Turn order by Speed stat — shown as queue at top of screen
5. Each turn: one character acts → Attack / Skill / Switch Stance / Item / Defend
6. Enemy attacks telegraphed 1 turn ahead in queue

#### The Momentum Gauge
Shared party resource. Three states:
- **GROUNDED:** Skills cost less, -20% damage, enemies slower. Safe but uphill.
- **BALANCED:** Full power, all skills available. Optimal.
- **SURGE:** +40% damage dealt, +30% damage taken. Risk/reward.

Filled by: dealing damage, offensive skills, crits, Vyn attacks.
Drained by: taking damage, Defend, Rynn's Crosswind, certain enemy abilities.
Enemies can actively push your gauge. Managing it is the core tactical tension.

#### Stance System (Ghost of Tsushima)
Each character has 3 stances. Switching is free, once per turn.
Enemy armor type shown clearly at battle start — no guessing.

| Armor Type | Weak Stance |
|-----------|-------------|
| Heavy/Armored | Crushing |
| Agile/Fast | Counter |
| Magic/Corrupted | Disruption |
| Spirit/Ancient | Knowledge |
| Shielded | Break |

Wrong stance = 60% damage. Right stance = 130%. Neutral = 100%.

#### Character Combat Roles

**ZI — Blade / Stance Master**
Highest single-target damage. No magic. Pure blade.
- Stances: Soldier's Edge / Counter Step / Iron Wall
- Starts with: Strike, Read
- Read reveals enemy armor + next attack. Evolves through story milestones.
- Full kit: Strike, Cleave, Read, Pressure Point (ignores 50% defense), Counter Stance

**CAELAN — Unstable Magic / Memory-Dependent**
High magic damage. Abilities evolve mechanically as his memory returns through story.
- Stances: Threshold / Gentle Current / Fractured Form (costs HP)
- Starts with: Divine Strike, Remnant Light
- Early: Surge Burst has 20% misfire. Story milestone stabilizes it.
- Full kit: Divine Strike, Threshold Hold, Fracture Touch, Remnant Light, Surge Burst

**SURI — Proactive Hybrid**
Every action deals damage OR amplifies next ally's damage. Never purely passive.
- Stances: Market Way / Harbor Guard / Open Hand
- Starts with: Quick Strike, Setup Strike (damages + marks enemy for +30% next hit)
- Full kit: Quick Strike, Double Tap, Setup Strike, Momentum, Stand Beside, Bond Strike

**RYNN — Weather / Indirect Damage**
Damage through lightning, wind, and conditions. Different vectors, always contributing.
- Stances: Static Charge / Gale Force / Pressure Drop
- Starts with: Lightning Strike (ignores armor), Pressure Drop
- Full kit: Lightning Strike, Static Field, Cyclone, Pressure Drop, Chain Lightning, Crosswind

**LEX — Precision / Knowledge Damage**
Modest raw damage but bypasses defenses. Makes every other character more effective.
- Stances: Field Study / Anatomical / Pattern Read
- Starts with: Pressure Strike (ignores 75% defense), Field Notes
- Full kit: Pressure Strike, Exploit Weakness, Pattern Break, Field Notes, Appease, Studied Strike
- Special: Appease can end spirit-type encounters without fighting (requires Lex in party)

**VYN — Wild / Reactive**
Partially autonomous. Always contributing damage. Passive: auto-intercepts when ally drops below 25% HP.
- Starts with: Wild Strike, Pack Instinct
- Full kit: Wild Strike, Root, Pack Instinct, Ancient Pulse, Communion
- Bond with Zi: when Zi below 30% HP, Vyn damage +50%

#### Party Combos (Affinity-Based, discovered through play)
| Pair | Trigger | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| Zi + Caelan | Zi uses Read → Caelan attacks | Guaranteed +50% vs revealed weakness |
| Zi + Suri | Suri uses Setup Strike → Zi attacks | Setup bonus + armor pierce |
| Rynn + Lex | Lex uses Field Notes → Rynn attacks | Lightning hits all weak points |
| Suri + Vyn | Suri uses Stand Beside for Vyn | Vyn retaliates twice |
| Caelan + Lex | Both in party vs spirits | Passive +20% spirit damage |

#### God Boons in Combat
Invoked before battle at shrines. Costs shrine currency.
| God | Boon |
|-----|------|
| Auryn | Reveals all armor types + next 3 enemy attacks |
| Morrael | One party member auto-revives once at 30% HP |
| Thessia | Top affinity pair gets one Bond Strike |
| Varek | Surge duration doubled, Surge damage taken -20% |
| Lyenne | One character gets a free extra turn, usable once |

#### Progression — Fire Emblem Unlock Curve
Start simple. Complexity is a reward, not a barrier.
- **Lv 1-5:** 2 actions per character. Clean, readable, nobody feels weak.
- **Lv 6-10:** Stances unlock one at a time. Enemy variety demands switching.
- **Lv 11-15:** Advanced actions unlock. Cleave, Chain Lightning, Pattern Break.
- **Lv 16-20:** Affinity combos, god-modified abilities, Caelan's Surge Burst stabilizes.
Every level feels like a gift, not homework.

#### What We're Not Including
- ❌ Grinding — milestone progression only
- ❌ Passive % buffs as primary rewards
- ❌ Status effect stacking (Disgaea problem)
- ❌ Purely passive characters (explicit rule: everyone damages HP)
- ❌ RNG without telegraphing
- ❌ Equipment as pure stat numbers (modifies stances and unlocks abilities)
- ❌ More than 5 actions per character at any time

### 3. Base Camps
- Scattered across world, discovered through exploration
- Each camp can develop over time:
  - **Forge** — weapon and armor upgrades
  - **Sanctum** — magic research, divine shrine access
  - **Quarters** — relationship scenes, bonding, romance moments
  - **Outpost** — fast travel hub, storage, world map
- Rest at camp to save and recover HP/MP

### 4. Relationships & Affinity
- Bond system per companion — builds through dialogue, camp scenes, gifts, shared meals
- Hades-style: characters react to your progress, choices, and failures
- Affinity levels unlock new story scenes and personal dialogue
- Romance available with multiple characters
- Inner monologue shows Zi's real thoughts vs what she says out loud
- Suri is bisexual — her arc is written with the same care as any other

### 5. Gods System
- Divine shrines at specific world locations
- Call on a god → receive help within their domain → give something back
- Each god has 3-4 specific interventions
- What they take: memory, affinity points, HP, a possession, a decision
- Overusing one god has story consequences
- Gods operate on outdated information — their help is genuine but sometimes off

### 6. Journal
- Zi writes after major events — unfiltered, handwritten visual style
- Auto-updates with lore, character notes, world discoveries, god interactions
- Readable at any time from the menu
